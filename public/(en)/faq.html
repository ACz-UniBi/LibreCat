Who do I turn to with questions on PUB?

Please contact the Support Team "Publication Services". They will reply to your enquiries, if necessary in consultation with the proper university departments (e.g. HRZ and local IT supervisors in case of login problems or the BIS Team in case of questions regarding the directory of staff and departments (PEVZ).

How do I register for PUB?

PUB requires the HRZ username and password. If you haven't received a personal letter, please contact the Support Team "Publication Services".
TIP: If you forgot your password, please turn to the local IT Supervisor of your department.

Can I customise my publication list?

After the login you will see an internal view where you manage your publication data. A preview of the list which will be displayed in public provides the link "Public View" in the navigation bar on the left. The option "Citation Style Settings" provides some styles which are widely used in particular scientific communities and thus lets you choose an appropriate and individual look of your publication list.
The short guide PUB in a Nutshell will help you with some basic information in this matter.

There are duplicates in my list. What can I do?

As we used several sources from which we imported the data in order to establish a sound basis for the users, you have to deal with duplicates indeed. And: as tastes differ when it comes to the quality of the records. So we wanted to offer different options for you to choose from. In principle, the data marked with "ISI" or "BiPrints" are high-quality and can be used directly.
The short guide PUB in a Nutshell will help you with some basic information in this matter.

I already have a classified publication list which is maintained by my department. Do I still have to do something?

We are already working on co-operative classified solutions with several departments (e.g. CITEC, CeBiTec, Biology). Please contact the Support Team "Publication Services" for an appointment, if you want us to coordinate efforts.
Even if you already maintain a classified list, please contact us so that we can coordinate or even match existing and new solutions.

What happens to the information about projects, theses, and science awards?

Displaying publication data as an essential means of external presentation of research is the first step in modernizing the research information system of Bielefeld University. After prior consultation with the departments, new and optimized services for information about projects, theses, and science awards will be established later on in this process if necessary.
